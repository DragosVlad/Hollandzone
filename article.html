Electronic Family festival is somewhat unknown to us but having a look at the line-up this festival is obviously not the new kid in town. With well established names in their line-up, especially for trance freaks, Electronic Family promised to be an exciting and electrifying day. This year celebrating their fifth anniversary.

Although excited but unsure what to expect we drove off to Amsterdam early on where this festival was held at “Amsterdams Bos”. Driving to the parking area went smoothly with sufficient traffic indications pointing us to the right direction.

Getting to the festival area from where we’ve parked was quite an adventure itself. A long walk through the forest which felt like forever but actually, with our pace, took around 20-25 minutes minutes. If you went by car and tried to cut down on walking, you could always purchase a premium parking ticket which was double the price (30 euros) but that provides you with an option to park spot on. Instead we cut down on the money and did a marathon.

Arriving at the festival location it caught our attention that most visitor arrived early on. At most festival it does not really get crowded after two or three hours. The weather, around 22 degrees and overall sunny, certainly helped a hand. The average visitor age at this festival appeared to be higher than usual, which made us felt young . Musicians like ATB, Kai Tracid, Cosmic Gate, Paul van Dyk, Marco V appear to attract a different, somewhat older crowd, compared to the more mainstream festivals/music.

Once passing the security check point we finally made it to the playground. Just at the beginning we see lots of people taking or having their picture taken at the Electronic Family art tree and writing down a lovely note. Everyone seemed noticeable thrilled to be here causing a great vibe.

Overlooking the festival area it appeared very spacious and playful. In the centre a chill out area and surrounded by stages, food, clothing stands and even a fair attraction. Once purchased the food/drink coins we continued our way towards the family stage (main stage). With Jochen Miller behind the wheels of steel the party sets off immediately. Dozens of people gathering around the stage and start moving by the beats pumping out of the speakers. Some of them with very familiar faces we’ve seen at the Downunder – and Festifoort festival. Not only Dutchies but also visitors from France, Germany, Italy, Down Under and even Beirut waiving their country flags proudly while dancing with a big smile on their faces.

In the tent, near the entrance, a legend stage was constructed with dj’s like Chicane, Kai Tracid, Marco V, Rank 1 and Scott Project spinning trance tracks. Towards the middle, under a metal construction, you could find the Ajunabeats stage with some one the finest techno tracks. This area was packed with techno crazed people. Somewhat off the main area a fourth stage “Grotesque” was laid out. Four stages on this playground felt like the right amount. A cozy feelin’ and no long distance walk between the area’s.

For the remaining quantifications: plenty of food and drink stands but not a broad assortment. Unlimited toilet visit for one coin which is a bit of a downside. But on the upside there were more than sufficient facilities. Another upside is that you could purchase a bottle for unlimited water refills for two coins.

At the main stage Paul van Dyk performed the closing and last fifteen minutes tons of fireworks lighting up the sky. A perfect end to a perfect day. We all had a blast and will certainly revisit Electronic Family next year!
